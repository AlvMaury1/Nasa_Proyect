About Uranus

Type Ice Giant

Lenght of a year (orbital perior) 84 years
Numbers of moons 27
Average distance from the Sun 2.9 billion km
Temperature Between -226 and -197 degrees Celsius

Due to its non-solid surface, landing or visiting this planet is impossible. However, visiting its largest moons like Titania and Oberon could be feasible, as they have a solid surface with ample exploration potential, featuring numerous ice-covered craters and mountains. From there, one could observe the planet's blue-green color along with its ring system

Extreme Tilt

Uranus is tilted almost 90 degrees in relation to its orbit around the Sun, causing it to rotate almost on its side. This results in extremely long seasons and a unique distribution of sunlight on its surface.

Uranus' Moons

Uranus has a total of 27 known moons, many of which are small and have names derived from characters in works by William Shakespeare and Alexander Pope. The largest moon, Titania, is one of the largest in the solar system.

Internal Heating

Despite its distance from the Sun, Uranus is believed to have an internal heat source that may contribute to its atmospheric activity and the formation of water ice in its interior.
