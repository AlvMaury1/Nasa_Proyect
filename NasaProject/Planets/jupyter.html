About Jupiter

Type Gas giant
Size(diameter) 139,822 km, or about 11 Earths
Mass 1.9 × 1027 kg, or 318 Earths
Lenght of a year (orbital perior) 11.8 years
Numbers of moons 79
Average distance from the Sun 778.6 million km
Temperature Between -161 and -108 degrees Celsius

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and also the largest in our solar system, Jupiter has at least 79 known moons. 53 of them have names, while 26 are not yet named. Jupiter completes a full rotation on itself in less than 10 hours. Galileo Galilei studied Jupiter in 1600s and discovered its four largest moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede and Callisto. A well-known feature of Jupiter is the Great Red Spot, a persistent anticyclonic storm. It was first observed in 1831. It is larger than the Earth. Mathematical models suggest that the storm is stable and will be a permanent feature of the plane.

Great Red Spot

This is one of Jupiter's most iconic phenomena. It's a gigantic storm that has been active for centuries. The Great Red Spot is much larger than Earth and is a long-lasting feature in the planet's atmosphere.

Jupiter's Rings

Though less impressive than Saturn's rings, Jupiter also has a system of faint rings primarily composed of dust particles. These rings are difficult to see from Earth and were discovered through space probes.

Little Red Spot: Alongside the Great Red Spot

there is another similar phenomenon known as the "Little Red Spot" or "Great Red Spot Junior." It is a smaller but still impressive storm.
