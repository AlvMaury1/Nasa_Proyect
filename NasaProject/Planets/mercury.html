Mercury

The rocky planet, Mercury, with 38% of the size of Earth and temperature between -173º and 427º degrees Celsius, has a surprisingly large iron core. Named after the Roman messenger of the gods, who was known for being very swift. Mercury takes only 88 days to complete one orbit around the sun, the shortest year of all planets, but it takes a long time to rotate on its own axis: one day-night cycle on Mercury takes the equivalent of 176 Earth days. Mercury’s atmosphere is so thin and fragile that it is instead considered an exosphere. Funny things: Mercury’s orbit looks somewhat egg-shaped and at first glance Mercury looks like Earth’s Moon. With its most captivating feature being the temperatures that can swing from scorching heat to freezing cold in a short span, you can also witness breathtaking sunrises
