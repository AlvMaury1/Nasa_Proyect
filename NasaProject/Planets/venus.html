Planet Name

The rocky planet, Venus, is the second closest planet to the sun and is often called Earth’s “sister planet” or “twin planet” because it has the 95% size of Earth. Venus rotation is called retrograde rotation which means: Venus rotation is clockwise. Venus takes 243 days on Earth to complete a full rotation on its axis and 225 days on Earth to complete one orbit around the sun. On Venus, as on Mercury, a year is actually shorter than a day. Venus shows evidence of very strong volcanic activity and estimates that 80% of the planet’s surface is covered by smooth. Venus has constant temperatures of 450 degrees Celsius and its clouds are filled with sulfuric acid. It has 2 main plateaus as continents, in the north the Ishtar Terra plateau with the Maxwell Montes mountain, 2km larger than Everest, Ishtar Terra is approximately the size of Australia. In the southern hemisphere is Aphrodite Terra, larger than Ishtar Terra and with a size equivalent to South America.
